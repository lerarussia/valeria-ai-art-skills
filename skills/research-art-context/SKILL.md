# Context Research for Artists

## Workflow

1. Define the exact place, site, institution, time period, project purpose, and research depth.
2. Build parallel lines of inquiry:
   - geography, ecology, infrastructure, and material conditions;
   - political, social, industrial, and cultural history;
   - living communities, languages, rituals, conflicts, and contested memory;
   - institution, programme, curators, prior projects, and stated values;
   - contemporary artists, archives, collections, and local initiatives.
3. Prioritize official records, archives, scholarship, local media, oral histories, and community sources. Use tourism summaries only as orientation.
4. Cross-check claims and dates. Mark contested narratives and gaps.
5. Distinguish what can be researched remotely from what requires local conversation, permission, or fieldwork.
6. Identify extractive framing, stereotypes, sensitive sites, representation risks, and stakeholders who should be consulted or credited.
7. Translate findings into artistic opportunities without forcing a concept.

## Output

Return:

- concise context map;
- annotated timeline;
- institution and stakeholder map;
- key themes, tensions, materials, and sites;
- relevant artists/projects without imitation;
- ethical and access notes;
- fieldwork questions;
- source list with direct links;
- several grounded project directions clearly labelled as proposals.

Never invent local testimony or present outsiders' interpretations as community consensus.
