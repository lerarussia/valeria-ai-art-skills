# AI Art Production Planner

## Workflow

1. Define the artistic outcome, audience, format, duration/scale, deadline, budget ceiling, available team, and quality bar.
2. Break the concept into testable visual, temporal, spatial, and interaction requirements.
3. Design the pipeline:
   - research and references;
   - source/data permissions and preparation;
   - model/tool selection;
   - prompt or conditioning system;
   - generation tests;
   - evaluation and selection;
   - compositing, editing, sound, grading, and mastering;
   - hardware, software, installation, playback, and backup.
4. Prefer the simplest toolchain that meets the artistic requirement. Record alternatives for unstable or unavailable tools.
5. Create milestones with approval gates: proof of concept, style lock, production lock, rough cut/prototype, technical rehearsal, final delivery.
6. Estimate labour, subscriptions, compute, equipment, fabrication, licensing, travel, contingency, and taxes separately.
7. Track risks: visual inconsistency, model drift, rights/privacy, harmful bias, service limits, render time, resolution, codecs, connectivity, venue conditions, and maintenance.
8. Define file naming, versioning, provenance, credits, backups, and handoff specifications.

## Output

Return a production brief, pipeline, task/WBS table, schedule, budget, responsibility matrix, technical specification, risk register, and next experiment. Clearly mark assumptions and ranges; never fabricate vendor prices or legal clearance.
