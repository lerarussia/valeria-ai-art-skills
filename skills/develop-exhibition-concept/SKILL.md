# Exhibition Concept Developer

## Workflow

1. Clarify the core question, audience, venue, scale, available works, resources, and non-negotiable constraints.
2. Separate theme from argument. Formulate what the exhibition proposes, not only what it is about.
3. Map works and materials by conceptual role: anchor, evidence, counterpoint, transition, atmosphere, participation, or reflection.
4. Remove redundant elements and identify missing functions.
5. Create spatial dramaturgy with entrance, orientation, chapters, thresholds, climax, decompression, and exit.
6. For each zone specify purpose, works, media, sensory conditions, interpretive text, technical needs, accessibility, and desired visitor action.
7. Test multiple visitor paths, sightlines, dwell time, bottlenecks, sound/light conflicts, safety, maintenance, and staff needs.
8. Align title, synopsis, wall texts, public programme, and communication language with the same argument.
9. Separate confirmed works from proposed commissions or placeholders.

## Output

Provide:

- title options and one-sentence premise;
- curatorial synopsis;
- section logic;
- work matrix;
- visitor journey;
- zone-by-zone plan;
- interpretation and accessibility strategy;
- technical and production implications;
- unresolved decisions and next prototype.

Avoid decorative scenography that does not serve the exhibition's argument.
